# **Strategic Research and Design Framework for the Digital Identity of Roel van Bergen: The Digital Zen Garden**

The transformation of roelvanbergen.nl is a transition from a static repository of opinions to a dynamic, atmospheric digital garden where the smoothness of code serves the richness of the written word.1 This framework outlines a requirement and design plan centered on the vision of the **Digital Zen Garden**, leveraging a high-performance stack of **Eleventy (11ty)** and **Tailwind CSS** to showcase Roel as a technical expert, coach, and professional speaker.

## **1\. Technical Foundation: The Shokunin's Stack**

The choice of Eleventy (11ty) and Tailwind CSS aligns with the "Digital Shokunin" philosophy: choosing tools that are lightweight, extensible, and offer maximum control over the "fingerprint of thought" visible in the code.

### **Static Site Architecture (11ty)**

1. **Performance First:** The site must aim for a perfect 100/100 Lighthouse score. Using 11ty ensures zero client-side JavaScript by default, which is critical for a "Zen" user experience that feels instantaneous.  
2. **Automated Asset Optimization:** Implementation of the eleventy-img plugin to handle responsive images and automated conversion to modern formats (WebP/AVIF), ensuring that high-quality "in action" speaker photos do not compromise load times.  
3. **YouTube Integration:** A custom 11ty shortcode for YouTube embeds using the youtube-nocookie.com domain. This protects visitor privacy while allowing for easy "showreel" management as your video library grows.

### **Visual Styling (Tailwind CSS)**

1. **Minimalist Utility:** Tailwind will be used to create a "classless" look—utilizing generous whitespace (*Ma*) and a strict, calming color palette without the bloat of traditional CSS frameworks.  
2. **Custom Zen Components:** Creation of Tailwind plugins or utility classes for "ink wash" borders and text. For example, using \-webkit-text-stroke or custom SVG filters to create a "bleeding ink" effect on headers.

## **2\. Speaker and Trainer Strategy: Building Credibility**

As a speaker and trainer, your website must function as a "Meeting Planner's Toolkit." It needs to answer three questions instantly: *What do you speak about? What value do you add? Can I trust you on my stage?*

### **Showcase Without Video (The "Early Stage" Strategy)**

Until your YouTube showreel is ready, build authority through:

* **The "Speaking Topics" Catalog:** Mouth-watering descriptions of 3–5 core workshops or talks. Each should outline the "Transformation" for the audience (e.g., "From Inner Critic to Inner Coach").  
* **Proof of Work:** A "Past Events" list with titles, dates, and locations. Since you have given talks in the past, list them as high-profile "Proof Points" to demonstrate you are not a beginner to the stage.  
* **Slides & Photos:** High-quality professional headshots or "action shots" of you teaching. If you have slides (e.g., on SpeakerDeck), embed them to show the visual and intellectual quality of your presentations.

### **Essential Speaker Features**

| Feature | Implementation | Strategic Goal |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Hero "Action" Area** | Large header with a photo of you teaching or speaking. | Immediate visual proof of your role as a trainer/speaker. |
| **Testimonial Sprinkles** | Short quotes from past attendees placed next to blog content. | Builds "social proof" that your content is valuable in real-world settings. |
| **Speaker One-Sheet** | A downloadable PDF summarizing your bio, topics, and tech requirements. | Makes the life of an event organizer easy; includes your contact info on every page. |
| **Speaking Inquiry CTA** | A prominent, frictionless contact form titled "Invite Roel to Speak." | Converts readers who are impressed by your blogs into potential workshop bookings. |

## **3\. Design Vision: The Sumi-e Digital Garden**

The site will be a "choreography of feeling," mimicking the transition one feels when entering a physical Zen garden.

### **The Interactive Hero: The Sumi-e Pond**

Using **p5.js** or a similar canvas-based library integrated into the 11ty template:

* **The Fish:** Animated Koi fish in a Sumi-e ink style. These fish represent the "Digital Shokunin"—always moving, always perfecting.  
* **Interactive Ripples:** Cursor movements create ink-wash ripples on a "Washi paper" background.  
* **Performance Note:** The script will only load if the user interacts with the hero area, ensuring the site remains "Lightning-Fast" for mobile users.

### **Typography: The Intellectual Voice**

* **Headings:** *Inter* or *Montserrat* for a modern, technical feel.3  
* **Body:** *Source Serif 4* or *Libre Baskerville* for long-form readability, giving your coaching essays a "literary" and professional weight.  
* **Code:** *JetBrains Mono* for technical snippets, grounding the site in your engineering background.

## **4\. Requirement Plan: The Implementation Roadmap**

### **Phase 1: The "Digital Garden" Audit**

Audit existing blogs on roelvanbergen.nl. Prune older posts and categorize remaining ones into the three pillars: **Engineering (The Engine)**, **Coaching (The Inner Voice)**, and **Zen (The Presence)**.

### **Phase 2: Technical Build (11ty \+ Tailwind)**

* Set up a "Bento Grid" homepage layout.  
* Build the interactive Koi pond as a standalone JavaScript component.  
* Implement the "Speaking" page with your topics, past event list, and speaker bio.

### **Phase 3: Launch and Outreach**

* Integrate a newsletter capture (beehiiv or MailerLite) to turn casual readers into a recurring audience.5  
* Set up your YouTube placeholders, ready for the first professional recordings.  
* Optimization for 2025 speed benchmarks and mobile-first accessibility.7

## **5\. Conclusion: Mastery Through Subtraction**

By choosing a minimalist tech stack (Eleventy \+ Tailwind) and a Zen-inspired design, the website will not just list your skills—it will *demonstrate* them. The clarity of the layout reflects the clarity of your coaching, while the interactive pond showcases your technical ingenuity. This unique vision positions Roel van Bergen as a leader who understands both the engine and the person behind the wheel.
