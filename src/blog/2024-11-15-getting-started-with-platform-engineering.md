Platform engineeringhas become one of the hottest topics in the DevOps space, and for good reason. As organizations scale their engineering teams, the need for a solid internal developer platform (IDP) becomes increasingly critical.

## What is Platform Engineering?

Platform engineering is the discipline of designing and building toolchains and workflows that enable self-service capabilities for software engineering teams. The goal is to reduce cognitive load on developers and increase their autonomy and velocity.

Think of it as building products for your internal customers — the developers at your company.

## Why Does It Matter?

Traditional DevOps often led to a "you build it, you run it" model that placed significant operational burden on development teams. Platform engineering aims to abstract away the complexity of infrastructure and operations, giving developers:

- **Self-service capabilities** — spin up environments, deploy services, and manage configurations without waiting for ops tickets
- **Golden paths** — opinionated, well-supported workflows that make the right thing the easy thing
- **Developer portals** — a single pane of glass for all developer tooling and documentation

## Getting Started

If you're looking to start building an internal developer platform, here are the key steps:

1. **Talk to your developers** — understand their pain points and friction points
2. **Start small** — don't try to build everything at once; focus on the highest-impact workflows
3. **Treat it as a product** — assign ownership, gather feedback, and iterate
4. **Measure adoption** — track usage and developer satisfaction

## Useful Tools

Some popular tools in the platform engineering space:

- **Backstage** — open-source developer portal from Spotify
- **Crossplane** — infrastructure-as-code with Kubernetes
- **ArgoCD / Flux** — GitOps continuous delivery
- **Port** — internal developer portal

Platform engineering is a journey, not a destination. Start with empathy for your users and iterate from there.
